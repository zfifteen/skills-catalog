Technical Brief: Adversarial Review of zfifteen/prime-gap-structure (as of 2026-07-12)
(A) Invariant Inventory
The program rests on a small, interlocking set of arithmetic invariants derived strictly from the divisor function τ(n) and the Divisor Normalization Identity (DNI):
E(n) = (τ(n)/2 − 1) ln n, with dual Z(n) = exp(−E(n)). Primes sit exactly at E = 0 / Z = 1; every composite sits strictly positive in E (negative excess in Z).
Core invariants (all formally proved in PROOF.md, with zero violations in exhaustive checks to 10¹⁸):
Direct next-prime rule (tautological but reframed deterministically): q = min{n > p : τ(n) = 2}.
Gap Winner Rule (GWR) / Interior Maximizer (universal): In any nonempty gap interior I = {p+1, …, q−1}, the leftmost composite w carrying the global minimum τ-value in I is the unique maximizer of the raw-Z function F(n) = (1 − τ(n)/2) log n. Proof via Ordered Comparison Lemma (for composites a < b with τ(a) ≤ τ(b) ⇒ F(a) > F(b)), left-tail closure by Witness Threshold Lemma + Short Divisor-Average Lemma (∑ τ(n) ≤ H(log N + 2) + 2√N over interval length H), and right-tail triviality.
No-Later-Simpler-Composite (NLSC) Theorem: Once the global minimum τ-value appears at w, no later m ∈ I carries strictly smaller τ(m). This enforces strict hierarchical first-arrival ordering of divisor minima inside every gap.
Universal bounded compression at Cramér scale: w − p ≤ max(64, ⌈0.5 ⋅ (log q)²⌉) for every nonempty gap. The bound is proved by case exhaustion (high-τ branches eliminated up to k=128, finite base q < exp(16) ≈ 8.9×10⁶ verified exhaustively, analytic closure for large q).
Prime-Square Proximity Theorem (proved 2026-07-05): When the GWR witness is a prime square (τ(w)=3 ⇒ w = r²), the same Cramér-scale offset bound holds by a modular collision argument: any larger offset forces a modulus-link overlap with earlier rough-number sieves that would have produced an earlier τ=3 composite.
These are pure divisor-arithmetic invariants; they hold independently of any global distribution model or conjecture.
(B) Boundary of What Is NOT Claimed
The repository is explicit and disciplined about its scope.
No claim on the Riemann Hypothesis (RH) or any unconditional equivalence. The docs/rh/ directory sketches a speculative PGS-to-RH reading path but keeps it hermetically separate from the proved core; the core theorems stand without RH or even the Prime Number Theorem.
No claim on classical Cramér raw gap size. The bounded-compression result controls only the prefix attainment of the GWR witness (how early the structural “interior maximizer” appears), not the full gap length q − p. Cramér’s heuristic envelope O((log p)²) appears only as the scale of the witness offset bound; it does not imply or prove that maximal gaps themselves satisfy O((log p)²). The program explicitly disclaims any such raw-gap upper bound.
No probabilistic or random-model claims. The Super-Signal / Twin-Prime Resonance universal implication (z(w) ≥ 4 ⇒ g = 2) was actively invalidated on 2026-07-09 with concrete counterexamples (e.g., p = 17 666 309, g = 8, w with z(w) = 4) and CE certificates; the surviving modular lemma (≥4 zeros on the fixed remainder vector ⇔ 30 | w) is retained but stripped of its former universal twin-gap force.
Any extrapolation beyond local interior structure is outside the claimed boundary.
(C) Where a Sophisticated Reader Might Still Smuggle Classical Random-Gap Thinking
Even after reading PROOF.md, a reader steeped in Cramér–Granville–Maier probabilistic heuristics can unconsciously re-import randomness in three concrete places:
Interpreting the Cramér-scale witness bound as validation of the random model itself. The O((log q)²) prefix bound matches the heuristic maximal-gap scale, tempting the inference “therefore gaps behave randomly.” This is smuggling: the bound is proved from deterministic divisor ordering + finite-base closure, not from Poisson arrival or independent sieving. It constrains where the structural witness must sit inside any gap, not the statistical distribution of gap lengths.
Treating τ(n) fluctuations as statistically independent. The Ordered Comparison Lemma and NLSC force strict left-to-right ordering of minima; there is no room for “later simpler composites by chance.” A reader who mentally replaces the leftmost-min rule with a Poisson point process for divisor minima immediately recovers classical random-gap statistics and can pretend the PGS invariants are merely a rephrasing.
Conflating witness compression with global gap compression. Because the GWR witness is provably early, one can mentally fill the remaining q − w tail with “random composites” and recover the old Cramér picture. The program gives no control over tail length; adversarial large-gap constructions (e.g., primorial sieves) remain fully compatible with the invariants. The recent Super-Signal invalidation should be a red flag here: the modular zero-count that once looked like a deterministic twin-lock is now known to permit g = 8+, yet readers may still assume the surviving 30 | w lemma “almost” forces twins in a random sieve.
These smuggling routes preserve the classical random-gap worldview while paying lip-service to the deterministic machinery.
